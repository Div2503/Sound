How Sound Travels?

 Introduction :

  • When an object vibrates back and forth in air, then the molecules of air close to this object also start vibrating back and forth with same frequency.

  • These air molecules ,due to the direct contact with other layer of air molecules, pass on their motion to the other layer of air molecules. This makes that layer of air molecules to also start vibrating back and forth.

  • This process keep going and ultimately all the air molecules present in that area start vibrating back and forth.

  • When these vibrations reach our ears, our ears feels these vibrations as sound.

  • When a sound is produced by our vocal cords, then these vibrations are carried by air molecules using the steps given above and in the end, when it reach other person's ear, their ear catches these vibrations and send them to brain as sound.

  • This process of transfer of vibrations or transmission of sound is named as propogation of sound.



 Sound needs medium for Propogation :


  • The substance through which sound travels is known as medium.

  • This medium can be either solid, liquid or gas.

  • This means sound can travel by either solid, liquid or gas. But the exception here is that sound cannot travel through vacuum ( empty space with no matter ).

  • This is because in solid, liquid and gas molecules are present which have the ability to carry sound waves from one place to other whereas in vacuum no molecules are present to transfer sound waves.

  • This is the same reason why there is no sound in space because there is no air present in space to carry sound. Moreover, celestial bodies in space are not in direct contact to each other

  • In conclusion, we can say that sound needs medium for propogation ( transmission of sound ).

 Facts about sound travel :


  • Speed of sound in different mediums is mentioned in the below table :

    • Medium Speed
    1. Air 346 m/s
    2. Water 1498 m/s
    3. Iron or Steel 5000 m/s

  • Sound Travels fastest in solids, then liquids and slowes in gases.

  • Light is way faster than sound. For example, speed of sound in air is 346 m/s but speed of light in air is 300,000,000 m/s. This means light is almost a million times faster than sound.

  • If we imagine air molecules as balls, so when they are carrying the vibrations, there are areas where they lie together and some areas they lie away from one another.

  • The places where they lie together are called compressions and areas where they lie away from one other are known as rarefactions.
